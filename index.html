About
I am a PhD candidate in Economics at The New School for Social Research, specializing in machine learning applications to macroeconomic modeling and economic dynamics. My research integrates deep reinforcement learning, scientific machine learning, and statistical inference to better understand economic fluctuations and inform policy design.

I have experience teaching econometrics and machine learning to both undergraduate and graduate students, and I have contributed to international research and consulting projects with institutions such as the UNDP and the Centre for Economic Policy. My work is grounded in both theoretical rigor and practical application, with a strong interest in data-driven approaches to public policy and macroeconomic forecasting.

Technically, I am proficient in Python, Stata, and LaTeX, and I enjoy developing models that translate economic insight into actionable policy recommendations. I am especially interested in interdisciplinary collaborations at the intersection of economics, data science, and public policy.
Research
  • Macroeconomic Modeling with Machine Learning: Integrating deep learning and reinforcement learning into macroeconomic models to study economic dynamics and policy impacts.
  • Scientific Machine Learning: Applying advanced ML techniques to improve forecasting and inference in economics.
  • Policy Analysis: Collaborating with international organizations to develop data-driven policy recommendations.
Teaching

  • Workshops: Data science and programming workshops for students and professionals.
